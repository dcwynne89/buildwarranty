📌 Warranty Tips That Actually Make a Difference

Always put your warranty in writing

A verbal warranty is only as good as the memory behind it. Written warranties create a clear record for both parties. If a claim arises two years later, the document speaks for itself.

Be specific about exclusions

Vague warranties lead to disputes. "Normal wear and tear" is not specific enough. List exactly what isn't covered: neglect, unauthorized modifications, acts of God, misuse. Clarity now prevents arguments later.

Include a clear claim process

Tell your client exactly how to file a claim: who to contact, what information to provide, and what timeline to expect. A smooth claim process is the difference between a happy repeat client and a one-star review.

Use sequential warranty numbers

WRT-001, WRT-002, WRT-003. Sequential numbering makes it easy to track warranties, reference specific jobs, and look professional. It also helps if you ever need to cross-reference with invoices or contracts.
